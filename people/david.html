David Fairen-Jimenez

Biography

Dr. David Fairen-Jimenez is the leader of the Adsorption and Advanced Materials group in the Department of Chemical Engineering and Biotechnolgy at the University of Cambridge.

Education

  • ERC Consolidator Grant, 2017
  • RSC Barrer Award, 2017
  • PhD, Chemistry, Universida de Granada (Spain), 2006
  • BSc, Chemistry, Universidad de Alicante (Spain), 2002

Honors and Awards

  • Royal Society University Research Fellow (URF). The scheme provides the opportunity to build an independent research career, 2012.
  • Marie Curie Intra-European Fellowship for Career Development (IEF). IEF funding is provided for advanced training and career development. The founded project is based on a personal career development programme agreed between the researcher and the supervisor at the host organisation, 2009.
  • First Prize to the doctoral thesis by the Spanish Carbon Group. This prize acknowledges the best research work in the area every two years, 2007.
  • Postdoctoral Fellowship from Strategic Plan of the Universidad de Granada, Spain, to work two years at The University of Edinburgh, UK. This fellowship is given to highly qualified, experienced, researchers giving them an opportunity to acquire new research skills and experience working in new sectors, 2007.
  • Internship at the Laboratoire de Spectrométrie Physique, Université Joseph Fourier, and at the ESRF in Grenoble, France, financed by the Ministry of Science and Technology, Spain. Short fellowships for short visits in competitive institutes to learn new skills, 2004.
  • PhD Scholarship from the FPI program of the Spanish Ministry of Science. The FPI program is one of the two main scholarships funded by the Spanish government, and sponsors about 1,000 students every year. Around 10 % corresponds to Chemistry, 2002.
