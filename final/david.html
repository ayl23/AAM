David Fairen-Jimenez

David
Fairen-Jimenez Principal Investigator

Connect with me

I joined the department as a Royal Society - University Research Fellow at the end of 2012, a position that gave me the opportunity to develop my independent research pathway. Back in 2006, I did my PhD at the University of Granada, one of the most amazing cities in Spain, where I was working with monolithic nanoporous aerogels. I was born in Madrid not too long ago, and I always had an interest in science and research, although at that time I was primarily attracted by molecular biology and neuroscience. After my PhD (in Chemistry) I moved to the University of Edinburgh, where I started combining molecular modelling with my experimental background – I had two good fellowships at that time, including a Marie Curie, which allowed me to start developing my own ideas. I continued expanding my research at Northwestern University (USA), working with some of the strongest international leaders in my field, including Profs. Randall Snurr, J. Hupp and F. Stoddart – a Nobel laureate in Chemistry this year! I started in Clare College as a CRA when I arrived to Cambridge, before joining Robinson College in 2015, where I am now fellow and DoS.

I am married and have two happy children that allow me to get distracted from research. I love cooking, baking bread, good wine and enjoying small gifts of life; I started recently brewing beer at home, which opens a new direction in the kitchen. I used in the past to do kendo, archery and hill walking, and I hope I will be able to re-take them in the future.

You will be able to find me in my office in the south-east corner of the top floor of the new building.

Honors and Awards

  • Royal Society University Research Fellow (URF). The scheme provides the opportunity to build an independent research career, 2012.
  • Marie Curie Intra-European Fellowship for Career Development (IEF). IEF funding is provided for advanced training and career development. The founded project is based on a personal career development programme agreed between the researcher and the supervisor at the host organisation, 2009.
  • First Prize to the doctoral thesis by the Spanish Carbon Group. This prize acknowledges the best research work in the area every two years, 2007.
  • Postdoctoral Fellowship from Strategic Plan of the Universidad de Granada, Spain, to work two years at The University of Edinburgh, UK.
